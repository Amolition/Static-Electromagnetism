Gauss's law for magnetism can be expressed as : $$ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0$$ Where $\nabla$ is the divergence operator. Geometrically, the divergence of a field is represented by the net number of field lines moving in or out of a volume element. Gauss's law states that magnetic field lines do not have a beginning or ending but they form loops or extend to infinity.
