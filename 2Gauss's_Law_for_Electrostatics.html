Gauss's law for electrostatics can be expressed as : $$ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_{0}}$$ Where $\nabla$ is the divergence operator. A simple way to visualise this law is through electric field lines. Geometrically, the divergence of a field is represented by the net number of field lines moving in or out of a volume element. So Gauss's law states that the number of field lines moving in or out of a volume element is proportional to the charge density inside the element.
